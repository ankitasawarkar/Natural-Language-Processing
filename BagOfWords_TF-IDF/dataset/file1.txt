Lal Bahadur Shastri was born on October 2, in the year 1904, in the erstwhile Mughalsarai.
He was a freedom fighter who was deeply influenced by Gandhiji.
Lal Bahadur Shastri promoted the White revolution to increase the dairy and milk production in the country.
He also started the green revolution that aimed to improve the lives of farmers and increase the production of food grains. 
He became the second Prime Minister of India in 1964.
He gave the inspiring slogan “Jai Jawaan, Jai Kisaan”.
He died on January 11, 1966, in Tashkent, Uzbekistan.
A memorial was built for him in New Delhi, called ‘Vijay Ghat’.
He was a recipient of the highest civilian award, Bharat Ratna, which was given to him posthumously.
He is highly regarded for his honesty and simplicity and firm leadership during the Indo-Pak war.
